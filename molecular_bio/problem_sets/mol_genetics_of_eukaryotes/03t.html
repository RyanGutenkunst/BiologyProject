Problem 3: Why do pre-mRNAs get smaller during RNA processing?

The ovalbumin gene
The ovalbumin gene has been extensively studied. This gene is known to have 8 exons which encode the mature ovalbumin mRNA coding sequences. These 8 exons are interrupted by 7 introns, which are noncoding sequences that are transcribed into the ovalbumin precursor mRNA. During splicing of the pre-mRNA in the nucleus, the 7 introns, which account for 5828 (76%) of the pre-mRNA, are removed and the exons are joined together to yield a mature mRNA of length 1872 nucleotides.
