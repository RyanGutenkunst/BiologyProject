Problem 13. Monoclonal antibodies

Correct!

Within the body, a B cell with a specific antibody can undergo clonal expansion (make many copies of itself) in the absence of antigen.

A. True
B.

False

B cells can only proliferate and make a strong immune response if helper T cells give activation signals. Without the helper T cells, B cells can response only to a limited number of antigens, usually those with repeating structures such as a bacterial cell wall polysaccharide.
