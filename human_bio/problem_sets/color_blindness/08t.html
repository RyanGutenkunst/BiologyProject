X chromosome inheritance
The X chromosomes are inherited from either maternal grandparent and paternal grandmother The diagram shows how the X and Y chromosomes are inherited from the maternal and paternal grandparents to the parents to the son.

The Y chromosome is passed strictly from the father to male children in each generation. No male from the father's side of the family can be a source of genes on a women's X chromosome.
