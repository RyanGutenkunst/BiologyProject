Inheritance of RFLP markers

Humans have a total of 23 pairs of chromosomes. Each pair contains one chromosome from mom and one from dad. The RFLP markers most commonly used for DNA profile analysis are found on chromosomes 1, 2, 4, 5, 10 and 17. These RFLP markers are named after their locations on these chromosomes. For example, the marker on chromosome 2 is called D2S44 (section 44 of chromosome 2). These chromosomal locations are also referred to as DNA loci (from Latin: locus is singular, loci is plural).
