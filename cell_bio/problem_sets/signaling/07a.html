Problem 7: Second Messengers

Sending a signal through the cell membrane can lead to the production of second messengers inside the cell. Which of the following can serve as second messengers?
A. cAMP
B. Ca2+
C. IP3
D. NO (nitric oxide)
E.

all of the above

Yes, a large number of small molecules can act as "second messengers."

Second messengers are generated when a signal (ligand) binds to a membrane receptor. Cells are programmed for responses, and the same signal and second messenger can have very different effects depending on the programs operating in the cell.
